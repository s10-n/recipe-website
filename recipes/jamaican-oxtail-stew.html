Jamaican Oxtail Stew

[Ingredients] [Directions]
Tags: Jamaican, oxtail, stew, beef, hearty, savory, dinner, main course,
  • Serves: 4
  • Prep time: 12 hours
  • Cook time: 4 hours

Kirsten's aunt gave me this recipe, and it's my go-to for a delectable warm weather feast. I've braised pork shoulder using the same directions and it's turned out wonderfully.

Tips

Serve with Jamaican rice and cornbread. Navy beans or butter beans can be used.

Directions [^]

  1. Mix the dry rub ingredients in a bowl, and coat the oxtail. Once the oxtail is completely coated, dump the contents of the bowl into a plastic bag or other airtight container and refrigerate overnight.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350. Melt the butter and olive oil in a dutch oven.
  3. Add the oxtail and brown it on all sides. There should be a nice brown colour in the botton of the casserole dish.
  4. Once the oxtail is browned, remove it from the dutch oven and set aside. Deglace the dutch oven with the worcestershire sauce.
  5. Add the carrots and onion and cook until almost done.
  6. Add the green onions and cook until caramelized.
  7. Add the garlic, soy sauce, red pepper flakes, beef broth, ketchup, brown sugar, and oxtail. If necessary, add additional beef broth. Braise with the lid on for 3 hours.
  8. After 3 hours, take the dutch oven out of the oven and remove the oxtail. Set aside.
  9. Add in the cornstarch and water mixture, tomato paste, and beans and stir until mixed.
  10. Add oxtail and place back in oven for approximately 30 minutes or until the meat is almost falling off the bone.
